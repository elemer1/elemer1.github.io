- Don't make the mistake of judging your success based on your current peer group. They're not the benchmark for your life trajectory.
- Nobody is going to teach you to think for yourself. 
- Find vivid examples of success in the domains you care about. Eg, if you want to become a great scientist, try to find ways to spend time with good (or, ideally, great) scientists in person. Watch YouTube videos of interviews. Follow some on Twitter. Read their writing/commentary. Remember, proximity to excellence is contagious.
- People who did great things often did so at very surprising young ages, often in their 20s, sometimes earlier.
- The Single Most Important Thing to Do in Your Early 20s: Go Where the Standards Are Highest
  - He reflected on the book Apprentice to Genius, which chronicles three generations of scientists who mentored one another and ultimately achieved monumental success. Carefully deconstructing the lineage among these brilliant minds reveals that they passed down only two truly invaluable assets:
  1. Selecting Problems Worth Solving. In the scientific community—and indeed in any professional setting—you must master the art of selecting your targets. You are looking for problems that are sufficiently important, yet not so intractable that they leave you paralyzed and unable to move the needle. Identifying the right point of leverage is perpetually more critical than blind, brute-force perseverance.
  2. Witnessing True Excellence. The second lesson the apprentices internalized was understanding what constitutes a truly high standard.
  - This is also what Patrick considers the absolute core insight. When conversing with top-tier operators across various disciplines, a recurring phenomenon emerges: once you collaborate with exceptionally brilliant individuals or hone your skills in a premier environment, you instantly comprehend what true mastery looks like.
  - This experience unequivocally raises your baseline, fundamentally rewiring the expectations you hold for your own work. Once you have seen the ocean, you will never again be content wallowing in a puddle.
  - Therefore, regarding what one should truly do in their twenties, the optimal answer is perhaps this:
  - First, broadly identify a few fields of interest. Next, unearth exactly where the highest standards in those domains reside. Then, do whatever it takes to immerse yourself in that environment and experience it firsthand.
  - While you are young, expose yourself to the absolute highest caliber of work. This early compound advantage in perspective will serve as an enduring trump card for the rest of your life.
