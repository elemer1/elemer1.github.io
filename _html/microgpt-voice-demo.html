Click the button. The browser runs the model right now and produces 5 brand-new content seeds in this voice. Every click gives different output. Nothing is pre-recorded — you're watching a tiny AI generate text in real time on your computer.

Now generating in the PubMed academic voice

3Originality audit (the most important number for the lawyers)

For each voice, we ran 300 samples through an automatic check: how many appear word-for-word in the training data, how many are partial matches, how many are genuinely new content the AI invented. This is the version of "is the AI just memorizing?" that you can take to legal.

4What this is for, in plain business terms

Four concrete ways Compound's content team uses the same tool starting this week. Each comes with the actual ROI math.

USE CASE 01
Weekly automated topic seed machine
Monday 9am: one command, 4 minutes later we have 1,200 new content seeds spread across 4 voices. The content lead reads, picks 5–10, and the writing week begins with no "what should we write" friction.
ROI: 5 content leads × 1 hr/wk saved × $80/hr × 52 wks = ≈ $20,800/year, after $0 development cost.
USE CASE 02
"Who do we sound like?" — quantified
Train a 5th model on Compound's existing content. Compute its statistical distance to PubMed / Podcast / Reddit / Centenarian. Now "is our voice too academic?" stops being a feeling and starts being a number on a chart.
Strategic: turns subjective brand debates into data-backed editorial decisions.
USE CASE 03
Voice switcher for content reuse
Have a serious scientific draft? Feed its keywords to the Podcast voice — get 50 Huberman-style hooks. Feed the same to Reddit voice — get 50 community-style questions. One asset, multiple distribution shapes.
3–5× content reuse rate without paying for additional copywriters.
USE CASE 04
Compliance & originality moat
Every title generated is auto-tagged with its originality score. We can prove to legal, to readers, and to search engines: 96.7%–100% of our AI-assisted output does not exist in our training data. Most competitors using GPT-4 cannot make this claim.
Defensible IP posture in an industry where AI content lawsuits are accelerating.

5Three roads from here

Same technology, three different commercial reaches. Tier A is already done. Tiers B and C are choices about how far we want to take it.

Each market vertical (health, finance, legal, education, B2B SaaS) is a potential customer segment.
